# OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030)

## Exposure Control for Healthcare and Allied Settings

Last Updated: November 18, 2025

---

## What It Covers
Protects workers from exposure to blood and other potentially infectious materials (OPIM). Requires an Exposure Control Plan, engineering/work practice controls, PPE, hepatitis B vaccination offer, training, and post-exposure evaluation and follow-up.

## Required Program Elements
1. Written Exposure Control Plan (reviewed and updated at least annually)
2. Exposure determination (job classifications and tasks)
3. Engineering controls: sharps with engineered sharps injury protections (SESIPs), sharps containers
4. Work practice controls: hand hygiene, no-recapping rules, safe specimen handling
5. Personal protective equipment (PPE)
6. Hepatitis B vaccination offered at no cost to employees
7. Post-exposure evaluation and follow-up (source testing, employee testing, prophylaxis)
8. Training at initial assignment and at least annually
9. Recordkeeping: training, sharps injury log (where applicable), medical records confidentiality

---

## Sharps Injury Prevention Focus
- Evaluate safer devices annually with non-managerial staff input
- Monitor injury trends and implement corrective actions

---

## Documentation & Evidence
- Exposure Control Plan with roles/responsibilities
- Device evaluation records
- Training rosters/materials
- Vaccination declination or acceptance logs
- Post-exposure protocols and incident reports
