## Accreditation Process

### Survey Cycle
**Frequency:** Typically every 3 years (varies by program)  
**Types:**

- On-site surveys
- Focused surveys (for specific issues)
- Unannounced surveys (random selection)

### Survey Components

1. **Tracer Methodology**
   - Follow patient through care journey
   - Assess actual care delivery
   - Evaluate system processes

2. **System Tracers**
   - Data management
   - Infection control
   - Medication management

3. **Standards Compliance**
   - Review policies and procedures
   - Interview staff
   - Observe practices

### Accreditation Decisions
- **Accredited:** Full compliance
- **Accredited with Follow-up:** Minor deficiencies, requires evidence of correction
- **Preliminary Denial:** Significant deficiencies, on-site follow-up survey
- **Denial:** Serious patient safety issues

## Performance Improvement Framework

### PDSA Cycle (Plan-Do-Study-Act)
Joint Commission endorses rapid cycle improvement:

1. **Plan:** Identify problem, analyze root cause, design intervention
2. **Do:** Implement change on small scale
3. **Study:** Measure results, compare to baseline
4. **Act:** Adopt, adapt, or abandon intervention

### Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
Required for sentinel events:

- Credible and thorough investigation
- Identify systems issues (not blame individuals)
- Develop action plan
- Measure effectiveness of improvement
