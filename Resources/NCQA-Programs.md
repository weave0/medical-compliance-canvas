## Mission

## Health Equity Accreditation

**Program URL:** <https://www.ncqa.org/programs/health-equity/>

### Purpose
Recognize healthcare organizations demonstrating commitment to reducing health disparities and improving care for all populations.

### Key Requirements

#### 1. REaL Data Collection (Race, Ethnicity, Language)
- **Comprehensive data capture** at registration and all encounters
- **Self-identified data** (patient-reported)
- **Granular categories** per OMB standards
- **Integration into EHR** systems

#### 2. Stratified Quality Measurement
- **Analyze HEDIS measures** by race, ethnicity, language groups
- **Identify disparities** in clinical outcomes
- **Track trends** over time
- **Publicly report** results

#### 3. Quality Improvement Initiatives
- **Target identified disparities** with specific interventions
- **Culturally tailored programs** for high-need populations
- **Community health worker** deployment
- **Language-appropriate** patient education

#### 4. Governance & Leadership
- **Board-level accountability** for health equity
- **Equity goals** in strategic plan
- **Resource allocation** for disparity reduction
- **Diverse leadership** representation

#### 5. Community Engagement
- **Community health needs assessment** with equity focus
- **Advisory councils** from affected populations
- **Partnership** with community organizations
- **Co-design** of interventions

## HEDIS Measures (Healthcare Effectiveness Data and Information Set)

### Overview
**Usage:** 90% of U.S. health plans use HEDIS  
**Purpose:** Standardized performance measurement across healthcare  
**Update Cycle:** Annual

### Measure Categories

#### Effectiveness of Care
- **Diabetes Care:** HbA1c control, eye exams, kidney monitoring
- **Cardiovascular Care:** Blood pressure control, cholesterol management, cardiac rehabilitation
- **Cancer Screening:** Breast, cervical, colorectal
- **Behavioral Health:** Depression screening and follow-up, antidepressant medication management
- **Preventive Care:** Immunizations, well-child visits, adolescent wellness

#### Access/Availability of Care
- **Appointment Availability:** Adults and children
- **Prenatal Care:** Timeliness of care initiation
- **Postpartum Care:** Follow-up visits

#### Experience of Care
- **CAHPS Surveys:** Consumer Assessment of Healthcare Providers and Systems
- **Patient Satisfaction:** Overall ratings
- **Care Coordination:** Communication between providers

#### Utilization
- **Emergency Department Usage:** Frequency and appropriateness
- **Hospital Readmissions:** 30-day all-cause
- **High-Risk Medications:** Older adults

#### Health Plan Descriptive Information
- **Enrollment Data:** Demographics
- **Provider Network:** Size and composition
- **Medical Spending:** Total cost of care

### Digital Quality Transition
**Initiative:** Digital Quality Hub  
**Focus:** Moving from administrative claims data to electronic clinical data  
**Benefit:** More timely, accurate measurement

## Patient-Centered Medical Home (PCMH) Recognition

### Overview
PCMH is a model of care that strengthens the patient-provider relationship through:

- **Comprehensive care** across the lifespan
- **Patient-centered** approach
- **Coordinated care** across the healthcare system
- **Accessible services** (extended hours, same-day appointments)
- **Quality and safety** focus

### Recognition Levels
- **Level 1:** 35-59 points (foundational capabilities)
- **Level 2:** 60-84 points (advanced systems)
- **Level 3:** 85-100 points (comprehensive transformation)

### PCMH 2017 Standards (6 Core Concepts)

#### 1. Patient-Centered Access
- **Enhanced access:** Same-day appointments, 24/7 clinical advice
- **Electronic access:** Patient portal with messaging, prescription refills, test results
- **Cultural competency:** Language services, culturally appropriate care

#### 2. Team-Based Care
- **Care team** with defined roles
- **Patient involvement** in care planning
- **Comprehensive health assessment**

#### 3. Population Health Management
- **Data analysis** to identify care gaps
- **Outreach** to high-risk patients
- **Preventive services** delivery tracking

#### 4. Care Management and Support
- **Care coordination** for complex patients
- **Medication management**
- **Self-management support** (education, goal-setting)

#### 5. Care Coordination and Transitions
- **Coordinated referrals** with tracking
- **Hospital discharge follow-up**
- **Information exchange** with other providers

#### 6. Performance Measurement and Quality Improvement
- **Clinical quality measures** (HEDIS or equivalent)
- **Patient experience surveys**
- **PDSA cycles** for improvement
- **Utilize data** to drive improvement

### Health Equity in PCMH
- **Standard 1F:** Culturally and linguistically appropriate services (CLAS alignment)
- **Standard 6B:** Stratify quality data by demographics
- **Implicit requirement:** Reduce disparities through population health management

## Alignment with Other Standards

### Joint Commission
- **Complementary focus:** NCQA emphasizes health plans and ambulatory care; Joint Commission focuses on hospitals
- **Mutual recognition:** Some overlapping standards accepted
- **Coordination:** Both align with CLAS Standards

### CMS Requirements
- **Medicare Advantage:** NCQA accreditation satisfies many CMS requirements
- **Medicaid Managed Care:** States often require NCQA accreditation
- **HEDIS reporting:** CMS uses HEDIS measures for quality ratings (Star Ratings)

### CLAS Standards
- **PCMH Standard 1F:** Direct alignment with CLAS
- **Health Equity Accreditation:** Operationalizes CLAS framework
- **Multicultural Health Care Distinction:** Advanced CLAS implementation

## Implementation Roadmap for Organizations

### Phase 1: Assessment (Months 1-3)
1. **Identify applicable programs** (PCMH, health plan accreditation, health equity)
2. **Gap analysis** using NCQA self-assessment tools
3. **Review HEDIS measures** relevant to your population
4. **Establish baseline** performance

### Phase 2: Planning (Months 4-6)
1. **Set targets** based on Quality Compass benchmarks
2. **Develop improvement plans** for gap areas
3. **Allocate resources** (staff, technology, budget)
4. **Create timeline** for accreditation pursuit

### Phase 3: Implementation (Months 7-18)
1. **Staff training** on NCQA requirements
2. **System changes** (EHR modifications, workflows)
3. **Quality improvement projects** (PDSA cycles)
4. **Data collection** and monitoring

### Phase 4: Application & Survey (Months 19-24)
1. **Submit application** with documentation
2. **Prepare for survey** (if applicable)
3. **Address any deficiencies**
4. **Achieve recognition/accreditation**

### Phase 5: Maintenance (Ongoing)
1. **Annual data submission** (HEDIS)
2. **Continuous quality improvement**
3. **Re-recognition** (typically every 3 years)
4. **Expand** to additional programs
