## Definition

**Health Equity** is the absence of unfair, avoidable or remediable differences among groups of people, whether defined socially, economically, demographically, or geographically, or by other dimensions of inequality (sex, gender, ethnicity, disability, sexual orientation).

**Goal:** Everyone can attain their full potential for health and well-being.

---

## Social Determinants of Health

Health and health equity are determined by:

- Conditions where people are **born, grow, live, work, play, and age**
- **Biological determinants**
- **Structural determinants:** Political, legal, and economic systems
- **Social norms** and institutional processes
- **Power and resource distribution**

## Discrimination & Inequality Focus

### Embedded Institutional Discrimination

**Issues Addressed:**

- Stereotyping based on sex, gender, age, race, ethnicity, disability
- Prejudice in healthcare delivery
- Under-representation in decision-making
- Underserved populations

### WHO Response

- Systematic identification of inequities
- Elimination of structural barriers
- Progressive realization of right to health
- Policy reform recommendations
