# CMS Conditions of Participation (CoPs)

## Foundation Requirements for Medicare-Participating Providers

Last Updated: November 18, 2025

---

## Overview
Conditions of Participation (CoPs) are baseline health and safety standards that providers must meet to participate in Medicare. CoPs vary by provider type (e.g., hospitals, CAHs, ASCs, home health, hospice, ESRD).

---

## Primary Sources
- CMS State Operations Manual (SOM): [cms.gov/manuals](https://www.cms.gov/regulations-and-guidance/guidance/manuals)
- Provider-Specific Appendices (examples):
  - Appendix A – Hospitals
  - Appendix W – Critical Access Hospitals (CAH)
  - Appendix L – Ambulatory Surgical Centers (ASC)
  - Appendix Z – Emergency Preparedness (all provider types)
- Quality, Safety & Oversight (QSO) memos: [cms.gov/QSO](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/provider-enrollment-and-certification)

---

## Typical CoP Domains (Hospitals)
- Governing body & Medical staff
- Nursing services & Patient rights
- Quality assessment & performance improvement (QAPI)
- Infection prevention & Control/Antibiotic stewardship
- Pharmacy services
- Physical environment & Life safety
- Emergency services & EMTALA interface
- Discharge planning & Care coordination

---

## Practical Approach to CoPs Readiness
1. Identify applicable provider-type appendix and tag list.
2. Perform a gap assessment against each tag; capture evidence.
3. Prioritize high-risk/high-impact deficiencies (patient safety, immediate jeopardy potential).
4. Implement corrective action plans with owners and due dates.
5. Validate via mock surveys and tracer methodology.
6. Maintain continuous readiness: dashboards, huddles, leadership rounding.

---

## Crosswalks & Accreditation
Many organizations use deemed-status accreditation (e.g., The Joint Commission, DNV). Maintain a crosswalk from accreditation standards to CoP tags to demonstrate equivalency and continuous compliance.
